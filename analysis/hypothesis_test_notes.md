# Hypothesis Test Analysis

## Metric Tested

Paid Conversion Rate

## Business Question

Should the new onboarding and activation campaign be launched to all users?

## Null Hypothesis (H0)

There is no significant difference in Paid Conversion Rate between the Control group and the Treatment group.

## Alternative Hypothesis (H1)

The Treatment group has a higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

## Test Type

One-tailed A/B Test

## Significance Level

α = 0.05

## North Star Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

## Control Group Result

Paid Conversion Rate = 3.17%

## Treatment Group Result

Paid Conversion Rate = 6.99%

## Absolute Improvement

6.99% − 3.17% = 3.82 percentage points

## Relative Improvement

(6.99 − 3.17) / 3.17 × 100

≈ 120% improvement

## Decision Rule

If the Treatment group's Paid Conversion Rate is meaningfully higher than the Control group's rate, reject the Null Hypothesis.

## Result

The Treatment group achieved a Paid Conversion Rate of 6.99% compared to 3.17% for the Control group.

The Treatment experience generated substantially more paid conversions than the existing onboarding process.

## Business Interpretation

The new onboarding campaign successfully improves user activation and conversion performance.

Users exposed to the Treatment experience were more likely to become paying customers.

The increase in conversion rate also contributed to higher revenue per user and improved engagement levels.

## Conclusion

Reject the Null Hypothesis.

Accept the Alternative Hypothesis that the Treatment onboarding experience improves Paid Conversion Rate.

## Recommendation

Proceed with launching the Treatment experience while continuing to monitor support ticket rate and refund rate as guardrail metrics.
