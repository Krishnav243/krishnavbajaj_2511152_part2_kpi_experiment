# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The objective of this experiment was to evaluate whether the new onboarding and activation campaign improves user conversion and early engagement compared to the existing onboarding experience.

The analysis shows that the Treatment group outperformed the Control group across nearly all key business metrics. Paid Conversion Rate, the selected North Star Metric, increased substantially, while engagement and revenue metrics also improved.

Based on the experiment results, the Treatment experience should be launched to all users while continuing to monitor key guardrail metrics.

---

## North Star Metric

**Paid Conversion Rate**

Paid Conversion Rate was selected as the North Star Metric because the primary objective of the onboarding campaign is to increase the number of users who become paying customers.

This metric directly impacts business growth, revenue generation, and customer acquisition efficiency.

---

## KPI Tree Explanation

The KPI Tree was designed around Paid Conversion Rate as the primary success metric.

Primary KPI Drivers:

1. Landing Page Performance

   * Landing Page Visit Rate
   * Trial Start Rate

2. Onboarding Success

   * Onboarding Completion Rate
   * Engagement Score

3. Revenue Growth

   * Average Revenue Per User
   * Average Revenue Per Converted User

4. Guardrail Metrics

   * Refund Rate
   * Support Ticket Rate
   * Days to Convert

These metrics collectively explain how users progress through the onboarding funnel and contribute to conversion outcomes.

---

## Experiment Result Summary

| Metric                     | Control | Treatment |
| -------------------------- | ------- | --------- |
| User Count                 | 693     | 715       |
| Landing Page Visit Rate    | 64%     | 73%       |
| Trial Start Rate           | 25.11%  | 29.09%    |
| Onboarding Completion Rate | 15.58%  | 21.26%    |
| Paid Conversion Rate       | 3.17%   | 6.99%     |
| Average Revenue Per User   | 51.75   | 53.88     |
| Average Engagement Score   | 57.03   | 62.93     |
| Average Days to Convert    | 8.86    | 6.40      |

The Treatment group demonstrated consistent improvement across the user journey and generated stronger business outcomes.

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no meaningful difference in Paid Conversion Rate between Control and Treatment.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
The Treatment onboarding experience improves Paid Conversion Rate.

The Treatment group achieved a Paid Conversion Rate of 6.99% compared to 3.17% for the Control group.

This represents approximately a 120% relative improvement in conversion performance.

The results support rejecting the Null Hypothesis and accepting the Alternative Hypothesis.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

While conversion performance improved significantly, guardrail metrics were also evaluated to identify potential risks.

### Refund Rate

Control: 0.00%

Treatment: 0.42%

A small increase was observed, but the overall rate remains low.

### Support Ticket Rate

Control: 0.219

Treatment: 0.372

Support requests increased in the Treatment group, suggesting that some users may require additional guidance during onboarding.

### Days to Convert

Control: 8.86 Days

Treatment: 6.40 Days

The Treatment experience reduced the time required for users to become paying customers.

Overall, the guardrail metrics do not present sufficient risk to outweigh the observed conversion improvements.

---

## Segment-Level Insights

### Region Analysis

The Treatment experience showed improved engagement across all major regions.

### Device Analysis

Desktop and Mobile users responded positively to the new onboarding experience, with higher engagement scores observed in the Treatment group.

### Traffic Source Analysis

Referral and Organic traffic sources demonstrated strong performance improvements under the Treatment experience.

These findings indicate that the campaign performs consistently across multiple user segments.

---

## Final Recommendation

### Recommendation: Launch

The Treatment onboarding experience should be launched to all users.

Reasons:

* Higher Landing Page Visit Rate
* Higher Trial Start Rate
* Higher Onboarding Completion Rate
* Significantly Higher Paid Conversion Rate
* Improved User Engagement
* Increased Revenue Per User
* Faster Conversion Time

Although support ticket volume increased slightly, the business benefits substantially outweigh the observed risks.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* Some missing values were identified in a small number of records.
* Support ticket rates increased in the Treatment group.
* Refund requests increased slightly.
* Results are based on the observed experiment period and may vary over longer time horizons.

---

## Conclusion

The experiment successfully achieved its objective of improving user conversion and engagement.

The Treatment experience delivered meaningful business value and is recommended for full deployment.
